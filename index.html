India News Summary - July 19, 2025

1. Politics & Government

  • INDIA Bloc to Hold Key Online Meeting: Opposition parties are convening virtually to strategize on issues for the upcoming Monsoon Session of Parliament. This meeting signals continued efforts by the opposition to present a united front against the ruling government.
  • Government Calls All-Party Meet Ahead of Monsoon Session: The government has scheduled an all-party meeting on July 20, 2025, to discuss the agenda for the Monsoon Session, which begins on July 21, 2025. This is a standard parliamentary practice to ensure smooth functioning and address concerns before the session.

2. Economy & Business

  • India-US Interim Trade Deal Talks Conclude: India and the US have wrapped up a round of trade negotiations, aiming for a quick ‘mini deal’ before potential new tariffs. This indicates ongoing efforts to strengthen economic ties and mitigate trade barriers.
  • Central Bank of India Reports 33% Net Profit Growth: The state-owned Central Bank of India announced a significant 33% increase in net profit for the first quarter. This positive financial performance suggests a healthy trend in the banking sector.
  • India’s Investment Confidence Grows: A report indicates a strong 12.6% growth in investment confidence in India for Q3 2025, the highest among 32 economies. This highlights India’s attractiveness as an investment destination.
  • India’s Electronics Exports Cross $40 Billion: Union Minister Ashwini Vaishnaw announced that India’s electronics exports have surpassed $40 billion, an eight-fold increase over 11 years. This demonstrates significant growth in the electronics manufacturing sector.

3. Technology & Science

  • India to Unveil First Domestically Produced Semiconductor Chip in 2025: India is set to release its first self-made semiconductor chip this year, marking a significant step towards self-reliance in technology. This initiative aims to position India as a global player in semiconductor manufacturing.
  • India Validates Astronaut Training Protocols: India has achieved a milestone in its human spaceflight program by validating astronaut selection and training protocols. This development is crucial for India’s ambitious Gaganyaan mission.

4. Society & Culture

  • India-Mozambique Cultural Night Celebrated: The Indian Cultural Association of Mozambique organized its sixth cultural night in Maputo, showcasing Indian dances and songs. This event fosters cultural exchange and strengthens bilateral ties.
  • Protest Over Alleged Murder of Dalit Youth: A protest was staged at Jantar Mantar in New Delhi over the alleged murder of a Dalit youth in Haryana. This highlights ongoing social concerns and demands for justice.

5. Sports

  • India Enters Badminton Asia Junior Mixed Team Championships Quarterfinals: The Indian team has advanced to the quarterfinals, showcasing strong performance in the tournament. This bodes well for India’s future in international badminton.
  • Indian Wrestlers Win Gold at Budapest Ranking Series: Antim Panghal and Harshita Mor secured gold medals in wrestling, demonstrating India’s continued prowess in the sport on the international stage. This achievement adds to India’s growing reputation in wrestling.
  • England Defeats India in Women’s ODI: England won the second Women’s ODI against India by eight wickets, leveling the series 1-1. This result sets up an exciting decider in the series.

6. Entertainment & Media

  • Diljit Dosanjh Film Faces Ban in India: A new film starring Diljit Dosanjh is globally successful but barred from Indian release due to objections over a Pakistani co-star. This highlights the complexities of cross-border collaborations in the entertainment industry.
  • India Reports Over $1.1 Billion in Media Deals at WAVES 2025: India announced significant media deals at the World Audio Visual & Entertainment Summit (WAVES) 2025, indicating a booming media and entertainment sector. This suggests a strong future for content creation and distribution in India.
  • Indian Box Office Earns Rs 5723 Crore in First Half of 2025: The Indian box office recorded a 14% year-on-year growth in the first six months of 2025, with a total earning of Rs 5723 crore. This shows a robust recovery and growth in the film industry.

7. Geopolitical

  • India Reshapes Global Order Amid NATO Threats and Oil Deals: India is navigating complex geopolitical tensions, particularly concerning NATO threats related to its Russian oil imports. This indicates India’s strategic balancing act in a multipolar world.
  • India and China: Evolving Geopolitical Dynamics: External Affairs Minister S. Jaishankar met with his Chinese counterpart, highlighting the ongoing efforts to manage the complex relationship between the two Asian giants. This suggests continued diplomatic engagement to address bilateral issues.
  • India Advocates for UN Reforms: India continues to push for reforms within the United Nations, reflecting its growing influence and desire for a more equitable global governance structure. This underscores India’s ambition to play a larger role on the world stage.
